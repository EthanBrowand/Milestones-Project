What was the first device similar to a modern computer?

The first computer was known as the Analytical Engine which was invented by Charles Babbage back in the 19th century. Originally Babbage was working on the Difference Engine ,which was practically a glorified calculator, but he decided he wanted to improve it. Mainly he thought about improving its operation so that it could perform other kinds of calculations. He wanted the Analytical Engine to be able to perform any calculation it was given. He realised it had to have four key components:the mill(cpu), the store(storage & memory), and the reader and printer (input and output devices). The project was much more complex than any device built before it. In fact it was supposed to have more storage than any computer built before 1960.
